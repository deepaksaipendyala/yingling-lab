Research

Five interconnected research thrusts spanning AI-driven discovery, nucleic acid nanotechnology, soft and biomimetic materials, metal and magnetic nanoparticles, and sustainability — connected by multiscale molecular modeling at every level.

Our Approach

Multiscale Methods

01
Density Functional Theory
Electronic-structure calculations for ground-state properties, reaction energetics, and bonding analysis at the quantum level.
02
All-Atom Molecular Dynamics
Full-resolution simulations capturing atomic motions, conformational dynamics, and detailed molecular interactions.
03
Coarse-Grained and Mesoscale
CGMD and DPD approaches that bridge atomistic detail with macroscale behavior, enabling longer time and length scales.
04
AI and Machine Learning
Bayesian optimization, graph neural networks, and generative AI for data-efficient discovery and inverse design of materials.

Area 01

AI & Data Science for Materials

We design AI-driven workflows for materials discovery and optimization, coupled with rigorous data integration and data-fusion methodologies grounded in statistics and computer science.

Our approaches are specifically aimed at extracting actionable insight from small, sparse, heterogeneous, and multimodal datasets, and are transferable across application domains — from materials science to water quality and phosphorus sustainability.

  • Machine learning force fields for nucleic acids and nanoparticle systems
  • Graph neural networks and Bayesian optimization for inverse materials design
  • Generative AI for data retrieval and de novo materials design
  • Self-driving laboratories and autonomous experimental design
  • Materials data integration and knowledge-graph curation (RL-CURATE-KG, INTEGRATE-KG)
  • Handling small & heterogeneous data: imputation, augmentation, and multi-modal fusion

Area 02

Nucleic Acid Nanotechnology

We use multiscale molecular modeling to understand and design DNA- and RNA-based nanomaterials with programmable geometry, responsive properties, and tunable function.

Our work spans the self-assembly of nucleic acid nanoparticles, the dynamics and immunorecognition of DNA-driven condensates, and the development of machine-learning force fields that bridge atomistic accuracy with the time- and length-scales required for therapeutic delivery and supramolecular assembly.

  • Self-assembly and dynamics of DNA / RNA nanoparticles
  • DNA-driven condensates: topology, mechanical properties, and immunorecognition
  • Plasmonic chirality from protein–nanoparticle assemblies (Nature Materials 2026)
  • ML force fields for RNA: bridging atomistic accuracy and CG efficiency
  • Programmable supramolecular nucleic-acid assemblies for therapeutic delivery

Area 03

Soft, Responsive & Biomimetic Materials

We develop multiscale methodologies — combining all-atom MD with coarse-grained (CG) and dissipative particle dynamics (DPD) approaches — to predict the stimulus-responsive behavior of soft and biomimetic materials at scales and timescales that all-atom simulation alone cannot reach.

Our coarse-grained simulations resolve LCST coacervation, pH-response, polymer brush conformations, and morphology mapping in block copolymers and peptide–polymer amphiphiles. Our cellulose research — supported by the DOE EFRC Center for Lignocellulose Structure and Formation (CLSF) — has produced landmark structural models of plant cellulose synthase complexes by combining cryo-EM, crystallography, and atomistic simulation.

  • Reflectin proteins: structure, self-assembly, and proton conduction (PNAS 2020)
  • Elastin-like polypeptides (ELPs): phase separation, LCST behavior, coacervate structure
  • Cellulose & plant materials: CesA structure determination, cellulose synthases, biomimetic CO₂ capture
  • Block copolymers & amphiphiles: morphology mapping for peptide-polymer amphiphiles
  • CG & DPD simulations: mesoscale modeling of responsive polymers, ELPs, and reflectins where atomistic resolution is prohibitive
  • Polymer brushes: nanoparticle-embedded brushes, chemically tailored coatings, and adsorptive composites

Area 04

Metal & Magnetic Nanoparticles

The Yingling group pioneered all-atom simulation techniques for ligand-functionalized metal and magnetic nanoparticles. We create and parameterize empirical force fields for nanoparticle surfaces — enabling atomic-level characterization of ligand–surface interactions, nucleic acid–nanoparticle binding, and ligand stripping under environmental conditions.

We developed a novel all-atom method to model magnetic nanoparticle alignment under external fields, capturing Zeeman alignment, dipolar assembly, helix formation, and 2D/3D rotational ordering in different solvent environments — distinguishing our framework from coarse-grained alternatives.

  • All-atom NP force fields: novel potentials for gold, silica-coated gold rods, iron oxide
  • Zeeman alignment & dipolar assembly simulation method (JCTC 2022 cover)
  • Ligand stripping from nanoparticle surfaces (ACS Nano 2023; AMI 2025)
  • Plasmonic / magnetic Ni–Au composites by femtosecond-laser fabrication
  • Graphene-oxide models — large-scale, highly oxidized datasets and force fields
  • Multiscale modeling from atomistic to mesoscale for nanocomposites

Area 05

Sustainability & Environmental AI

We apply convergence informatics, molecular modeling, and machine learning to challenges in environmental sustainability — from phosphorus sustainability and biomimetic carbon capture to AI-driven risk modeling for water quality.

This thrust is anchored by the Yingling group's role in the NSF STC STEPS Center for sustainable phosphorus, and by collaborations with NSF, NC State, and external partners across water, environment, and energy programs.

  • Phosphate-binding peptides & materials for sustainable phosphorus recovery
  • Biomimetic CO₂ capture: carbonic anhydrase strategies and engineered enzyme–polymer composites
  • Functional hydrogels for selective phosphate removal from water and release on demand
  • AI-driven groundwater risk modeling: imputation methods for co-occurring inorganic contaminants
  • Knowledge-graph curation for environmental data (RL-CURATE-KG, INTEGRATE-KG)
